Salt and Pepper

by Howard Leoputra

25-Dec-2022

If you ask me, salt and pepper doesn't make a good combination. That's why I got salt on my bedroom floor and pepper sprinkling in the air. Just two grinders, one salt, one pepper and that's how I started the war against salt and pepper, and here's how it started.

×

It was another hot, ordinary, summer day (well I thought it was) and I was bored, so I went up the attic to see it there was something fun up there. Cobwebs, boxes, lamps, books, furniture, and dust, lots of dust. All that stood out was an old-fashioned ink pen glittering in the dim sunlight from the only window. The heat was overwhelming, so I took the pen, shoved it in my pocket, and climbed out.

I went in my cool, air-conditioned room and grabbed a piece of paper, and started to draw. I drew a stick pistol. 'looks ordinary' I thought, but I was wrong. When I put the lid back on, the stick pistol changed into a miniature, real, metal pistol. I picked it up and lightly touched the trigger. Bang! Bang! Bang! "What are you doing in there?" yelled my mother, "You said you were drawing!".
"Nothing!" I replied quickly and hid the pistol. I wonder if... I drew my two characters I created: Billy Bob, a weak, little, puny salt grinder with overpowered bodyguards and Sergeant Pepperson, a powerful and undefeatable hero to pepper grinders. He has weak bodyguards who only held pistols. I drew each side 5 bodyguards and capped pen.

They sprang to life. The salt bodyguards reacted first, "Get away from our boss," they growled as they drew their weapons. The first two had riot-shields and a tactical knife. The next one had a knight stick, a flash bang and 3 grenades. The other had an assault rifle, a desert eagle as a secondary weapon, and an emergency MP7. The last one was a shotgunner. Billy Bob wore a silly grin, a thick bullet proof coat, a crown and held a bag of extra-salt chips. The pepper soldiers were less active, "Boss, what should we do? Shoot them?" one of the said and fired a bullet. It bounced off a shield man's shield.
"I'll deal with it", said seargeant Pepperson, "Get away from our territory!"
"Your territory? Oh yeah?" challenged a salt grinder with a shield. Sergeant Pepperson held an axe 3 times sharper, heavier, and stronger than a lumberjack's would be. Pepperson dived at the salt grinder and swung the battle axe. It broke the riot shield in half.

Suddenly, the shotgunner shot a bullet that went through two pepper soldiers and making a few dents in Pepperson's armour. The armour consisted of give layers. Inside was a normal cloth shirt. Next was a thick tunic, leather pants, and leather cap followed with chainmail armour. Next was the iron chest plate, leggings and a knight helmet. Finally a tunic covered the rest with a cape flying behind it. St. Pepperson sliced the unguarded salt soldier in half, not before having a dagger pierced through the armour, stopping just half way through the chainmail. Chaos reignited. Pistol bullet flew through the air and salt and pepper flew everywhere through shattered glass. Gurnades destroyed armour and sent the pistols flying. After minutes of fighting, all that was left was Pepperson, a dying shieldsman, the assault soldier, and Billy Bob. The shields man stabbed his knife into Pepperson's leggings and burst into salt and glass as the soldier took his last pistol shot, threw it away and took out his MP7. Both sides had their own worries. Billy Bob was worried if he finished his chips, he couldn't get more. Pepperson was worried hist armour would survive. He took off his destroyed chest-plate revealing the chainmail that was starting to fall apart. The salt soldier used the last of his bullets, destroying the chainmail and shredding the inner tunic.

Suddenly, my mum called, "Come out now! It's time for lunch! I made spaghetti for you!" I put the pen down, rushed out, and slammed the door. That was the beginning of my problems.

I quickly dashed to the table and wolfed down my spaghetti. My mum raised her eyebrow suspiciously "What were you actually doing?"
"Um.. Well, uh, I was just drawing", I answered
"Well you got homework to do now", she said in an unconvincing voice.
"Well, about that, uh, I am kind of busy right now."
"Too bad! Do your homework!" she shouted
I truged to my bag and fished out my homework. Urg... The square route of 124 is 12. 8x32=256 Blah, blah, blah...

After about 900 quintillion years later, I was finally done. I walked back to my room. And when I opened the door, oh my! it was a mess. Shattered glass scattered on the table and floor. Salt was stuck in the carpet and pepper were sprinkling in the air. Each team had drawn more of the pens, at least 20 on each side. The Salt was drawing much slower on soldiers since about half were drawing chips and banners that said: "Billy Bob's the best." and had a picture an iron sword and painting to the enemy with hundreds of salt soldiers rushing past him at his command. The pepper side had just two thirds working on soldiers and the other third on food and weapons. "Stop!" I said. More pepper, salt and glass fell to the floor. "I said STOP!" I yelled at the top of my lungs.
The shooting stopped. Pens clattered to the floor. Banners stopped waving. Then outside the room, I heard my mum's voice, "What? Stop what? Who are you talking to? Anyways, can you help me with the dishes, sweetheart?"
"Behave!" I warned the shakers. "If you continue this nonsense, I will lock you all in one little box and take the pens away. Clean everything up. I have to go now, my mum is calling me. You have been warned."

The next two hours, I was too busy to check on the grinders. I made a mistake doing that. This time, they had swept away the mess but this time, they were fighting in an arena. The size covering up my bed and large desk. They built pillars so it would be stable. Thirty to a hundred and fifty soldiers were coming out of each side every round. spikes, fire barrels and mini-lava-pits were laged across the battlefield.
"We did what you asked," one of the salt banner holder muttered nervously, "Everything is clean."
"But what's with the fire and lava?" I asked furiously as I grabbed my bedside cup and splashed the water on the fire and lava. The fire was extinguished but the lava just cooled down a little and started turning into magma and rock.
"Umm..."
"Fine! I snapped," But you can only do this when I'm around.

The battle continued. The salt soldiers were smashing the pepper's even though there were just 3 of them in the battle against thirty-seven pepper soldiers. One of them were holding two colt 1191s, the second a medieval knight's sword, roman legionnaire shield and a German WW2 helmet. The third had a skull for its face and wore the cloth outfit for a soldier from the French Resistance. and summoned skeletons from the ground who were musketeers occasionally summoning French WW2 officers and some that had bayonets. The third was a necromancer. And the other team be Pepperson was shooting down all the necromancer units as the weak soldiers distracted the other two salt hero. Occassionally, the salt sent some of the super powerful soldiers to help the heroes. I got to admit it was pretty fun to watch, it was like a movie!
